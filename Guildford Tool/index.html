    Charlotteville Cycling Club
    Founded 27 March 1903 · Guildford, Surrey

    The Charlotteville Cycling Club is a historic and active cycling club based in Guildford, Surrey. Catering to riders of all ages and skills, the club has a vibrant culture that spans from casual group rides to high-level national racing.

    History & Foundations

    The club was officially founded on 27 March 1903 at the Charlotteville Institute on Addison Road in Guildford. It was initially established to provide a dedicated cycling club for "working-class cyclists" in the area, differentiating itself from other local contemporary clubs which had become increasingly middle-class or focused on the burgeoning automobile movement. While other early local clubs eventually disbanded, Charlotteville endured to become Guildford's primary historical cycling institution. Over the decades, the club has produced top-tier talent, including riders who competed in World Championship trials and set national time trial records.

    Club Rides & Disciplines

    The club runs an active year-round schedule, supporting road riding, time trials, track, cyclo-cross, and mountain biking.

    • Sunday Club Runs — the club's signature event is the weekly Sunday morning ride, a great entry point for riders wanting to experience group cycling. Departs from the Old Guildfordians Clubhouse in Stoke Park at 08:30 during summer months and 09:00 in winter.
    • Time Trials (TT) — Charlotteville has a deep-rooted time-trialling tradition. They organise an annual series of club time trials, primarily in the Normandy/Pirbright area, and participate alongside neighbouring clubs in a local inter-club time trial league.
    • Other events — mid-week rides, track sessions, and off-road excursions fluctuate throughout the calendar depending on the time of year.
    Winter Turbo Sessions
    • Weekly indoor cycling at Guildford Spectrum — Tuesdays, 8–9pm (60 min)
    • £5 pay-as-you-go, or £50 for a 12-session block
    • Contact Jonathan Dobson or Julian White; sign up via the Spond app
    Saturday Rides
    • Speed — non-stop, ~39mi (63km), year-round (alt. winter route); departs Stoke Park Pavilion 07:30; drop ride for confident intermediate/advanced riders — try a Sunday Club Run first
    • Medio — no-drop, ~47mi (76km), departs 08:30, coffee stop in Tilford
    • Social — occasional, gentler pace over the ~39mi Speed route, departs 08:30, coffee stop in Shalford
    • Routes, GPX files and sign-ups posted on Spond
    Evening Hills Ride
    • Tuesdays through summer 2026 (7 Apr – 29 Sep)
    • Meets at the Horse & Groom, Epsom Road, Merrow — 6:15pm start
    • Local hill reps, finished off with a drink; check Spond for weekly details
    Women's Rides
    • Tuesday evenings, summer months — 6pm from the Old Guildfordians Clubhouse, Stoke Park (London Road)
    • 1–1.5 hours, 20–30km, quiet lanes where possible, paced for the group
    • Open to all abilities, ages 18+
    • Bring: road bike, helmet (mandatory), front/rear lights, basic tool kit (spare tube, levers, pump/CO2, multitool), emergency contact details
    Coaching & Youth (Charlotteville Rascals)

    For younger riders, Charlotteville operates a highly regarded junior Go-Ride section known as the "Charlotteville Rascals."

    • Under-16s focus — designed for children and teenagers between the ages of 6 and 16.
    • Skills development — weekly coached sessions cover fundamental bike handling, safety, and group riding confidence, bridging the gap into youth competitive racing for those interested.

    Rascal Racing / Go-Ride races

    • Four races a year: Rabbit Run (Mar/Apr), Maillot Jaunt (Jun/Jul), Rainbow Splash (Sep/Oct), Reindeer Dash (Dec) — dates on the Rascals Calendar
    • Open to ages 6–16 (Guildford & Woking Rascals plus other Go-Ride clubs), split by BC youth categories: Youth E/U8, D/U10, C/U12, B/U14, A/U16
    • Venue: Goldsworth Park Recreation Area, Woking (road tyres not suitable)
    • Registration 9:00–9:20am, no late entry; small entry fee; parental consent form required
    • Format: Individual Time Trial, Skills Test, Just 4 Fun, Handicap Race — done by ~12:00 with presentations
    • Bring warm layers and snacks; parents welcome to stay and cheer
    • Keen on regular coaching sessions afterwards? Get in touch with the club
    Membership & Club Culture

    The club maintains a robust and welcoming membership of well over 200 riders.

    • All abilities welcomed — membership spans leisure cyclists, youth riders, and veteran time-trialists.
    • Club kit — members can represent the club in custom Pactimo kit, distributed via a member-only shop open a few times a year. A relaxed "Continental" fit is offered for endurance comfort, and a snug "Ascent" fit for racing.
    Connection to the Guildford Town Centre Races

    One of Charlotteville Cycling Club's greatest contributions to the wider British cycling community is its exclusive promotion of the Guildford Town Centre Races.

    • The blueprint — first organised in 1984 by club members Les Smith, Mike Willimott, and Stuart Campbell, the event was created to celebrate the club's 80th anniversary in tandem with the Guildford Festival.
    • The circuit — it has evolved into one of the most prestigious, fast-paced criteriums in the British circuit racing calendar: a gruelling, 1-kilometre loop featuring a punishing, technical climb up the historic, cobbled High Street alongside North Street and Market Street.
    • National recognition — the event sits as a marquee fixture of British Cycling's National Circuit Series, drawing thousands of spectators and elite professional domestic teams to the town centre every summer.
